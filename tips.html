Travel Tips

Packing Tips

Subway
  • Average cost is 1500won
  • 6 seat at each end of a subway car is for elderly or disabled people only.
    ->Do not sit there.
  • Pink seats at the end of a row is for pregnant women only.
  • Wait in line to get on the subway. Do not rush forward to get on.
  • Don't talk loudly on public trasportation
  • Avoid commute time which is roughly about 7:30-8:30 and 5:30-7:00
  • Use Naver map to search your destination.
  • The train can get rough
    -> Hold on to something!
  • If the person sitting next to you change seats away from you
    it's not being racist, it's not you, it's because they enjoy their personal space or like the seat on the far end. It's perfectly normal and they move their seats regardless of if you're a foreigner or a Korean.
Bus
  • Usually the seats that have a different color(yellow or pink etc) is for the elderly or disabled only.
  • Sometimes if you don't press the red button the driver may not stop at your busstop.
  • The ride can be rough, so hang on tight!
Taxi
  • It depends on the driver but it's the craziest ride of all! So put on your seatbelt.

General Business Hours

1/1 New Year's day
  • One of the most important holidays
  • 3-day celebration
  • Can see some fireworks
3/1 Independence Movement Day
  • Korean declared their nation's independence from Japan in 1919
5/5 Children's day
  • Children's writer and social activist Bang Jeong-hwan declared this day in 1923.
  • Celebrates and honors children
  • Zoos, National parks, Amusement parks are crowded with families
4/8(lunar calendar) Buddha's Birthday
  • Celebrating Buddha's birthday
  • Usually mid-May
  • Colorful lanterns
6/6 Memorial Day
  • Koreans show their patriotism by remembering the men and women who died in defense of the nation.
  • At 10am a siren sounds for one minute which people bow their heads in silent prayer.
  • Many houses hang the national flag.
8/15(Lunar Calender) Chuseok
  • Harvest festival; one of the most celebrated holidays
  • 3-day event
  • Heavy traffic due to visiting hometowns
10/3 National Foundation Day
  • Celebrates the foundation of Gojoseon, the first kingdom of Korea
  • 개천; Means opening of the sky
10/9 Hangeul Day
  • Celebrates the creation of the Korean alphabet made by King Sejong in 1447
12/25 Christmas
  • Large Christian population
  • A romantic holiday for couples

General Business Hours

  • Restaurants & Cafés: Varies by location (Some 24 hours)
